5 Best Free Chrome Extensions for Pinterest Creators (2026)

2026-07-17
#pinterest#chrome extensions#pinterest seo#creators

Pinterest rewards consistency, quality, and optimization. But manually checking image ratios, crafting keyword-rich descriptions, and analyzing which pins actually perform — across dozens of boards — eats hours every week.

These 5 Chrome extensions handle the tedious parts so you can focus on creating content that drives traffic.

Pinello is a free Chrome extension that scores Pinterest pins across 5 dimensions — image, board fit, title, description, and hashtags — in real time.

1. Pinello — Real-Time Pin Scorer & SEO Checker

What it does: Scores your pin across 5 dimensions (image quality, board fit, title SEO, description optimization, and hashtags) as you create it — directly inside the Pinterest pin-creation modal.

Why creators love it:

  • See a green/yellow/red score for each dimension before you publish
  • AI-powered rewrite suggestions for titles and descriptions (one click, copy to clipboard)
  • Image analysis runs 100% locally in your browser — your photos never leave your device
  • Works on pinterest.com and all country variants (ca.pinterest.com, uk.pinterest.com, etc.)
  • Free tier: 50 AI scores per month, structural scoring always unlimited

Best for: Creators who want to optimize every pin before publishing. Think of it as a quality-check step — one high-scoring pin outperforms 10 unoptimized ones.

Price: Free (50 AI scores/month). Structural scoring (image ratio, hashtag count, keyword overlap) is always free and unlimited.

🔗 Install Pinello on Chrome Web Store

2. Tailwind — Pin Scheduling & SmartSchedule

What it does: Schedule pins from any webpage with a click. Tailwind’s SmartSchedule algorithm posts at optimal times based on when your audience is most active.

Why creators love it:

  • Bulk image selection — schedule an entire blog post’s images as pins in seconds
  • SmartSchedule picks the best posting times automatically
  • Tailwind Communities let you share content in niche groups for organic reach
  • AI Pin Designer generates pin templates from your content
  • Analytics dashboard shows which pins drive the most traffic

Best for: Creators who batch-create content and want hands-off scheduling. Tailwind handles the when and where — you handle the what.

Price: Free plan includes 5 posts/month. Pro plan starts at $14.99/month (or $9.99/month billed annually) for 100+ posts.

🔗 Install Tailwind on Chrome Web Store

3. Save to Pinterest — Official Pin Saver

What it does: Pinterest’s own extension. Right-click any image on any website and save it directly to one of your boards.

Why creators love it:

  • Fastest way to curate content from across the web
  • Hover over any image → Pinterest save button appears
  • Works on every website, not just Pinterest
  • Lightweight (tiny file size, no slowdown)
  • Built and maintained by Pinterest — always compatible with the latest platform updates

Best for: Curators and researchers who collect inspiration, competitor analysis, or reference images from around the web. Zero learning curve.

Price: Completely free. No paid tier.

🔗 Install Save to Pinterest on Chrome Web Store

4. PinRadar — Hidden Pin Analytics & Viral Score

What it does: Reveals engagement stats that Pinterest hides by default — saves, repins, reactions, and a calculated “viral score” badge on every pin in your feed.

Why creators love it:

  • See saves, repins, and viral scores directly on pins as you scroll
  • Sort any board’s pins by performance (saves, date, viral score)
  • Hover popups with deep analytics on any pin
  • CSV export for external analysis and content planning
  • Batch download pin images/videos in HD
  • Runs 100% locally — no data sent to external servers
  • Free license key at pinradar.io unlocks all features

Best for: Creators who want to reverse-engineer what’s working. Find viral content in your niche, understand what competitors’ top-performing pins have in common, then replicate that formula.

Price: Free (sign up at pinradar.io for a free license key that unlocks all features).

🔗 Install PinRadar on Chrome Web Store

5. Keywords Everywhere — Pinterest Keyword Research

What it does: Shows search volume, trend data, and related keywords as you browse Pinterest. Type a search term and instantly see how popular it is and what related terms people search for.

Why creators love it:

  • Real-time keyword metrics appear in Pinterest search as you type
  • Hashtag generator for Pinterest (and Instagram)
  • Trend tracking shows keyword popularity over time
  • Works across 20+ platforms (Google, YouTube, Amazon, Pinterest, and more)
  • Free tier includes Pinterest engagement metrics and hashtag suggestions — no API key needed

Best for: Creators who want data-backed keyword choices for pin titles, descriptions, and board names. Pair with Pinello for keyword research → optimized pin creation.

Price: Free tier includes Pinterest metrics and hashtag generator. Paid plans start at $2/month for detailed search volume and CPC data.

🔗 Install Keywords Everywhere on Chrome Web Store

How to Build a Free Pinterest SEO Stack

You don’t need to use all 5. Here’s the 3-tool stack that covers the full Pinterest workflow for $0/month:

  1. Research → Keywords Everywhere (find high-volume keywords for your niche)
  2. Optimize → Pinello (score your pin against those keywords before publishing)
  3. Analyze → PinRadar (see which pins went viral, reverse-engineer the pattern)

Add Tailwind ($14.99/mo) only when you’re posting 5+ pins per day and need scheduling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do these extensions slow down Pinterest?

No. All 5 are lightweight. Pinello and PinRadar run analytics locally in your browser — no external requests while you browse. Save to Pinterest is built by Pinterest themselves and weighs almost nothing.

Can I use multiple Pinterest extensions at once?

Yes. They don’t conflict. A common setup is Pinello + PinRadar + Keywords Everywhere running simultaneously — each serves a different purpose.

Do any of these read my Pinterest password?

None of these extensions require your Pinterest login credentials. They work by reading the publicly visible page content (pin text, images, stats).

What about Pin Inspector ($67)?

Pin Inspector is a solid keyword research tool, but it’s a one-time $67 purchase and primarily a desktop app. If you only need keyword suggestions while browsing Pinterest, Keywords Everywhere (free) covers most of the same ground. Pin Inspector is worth it if you do deep competitive research or need batch keyword exports.

Start With Scoring

The highest-ROI optimization for Pinterest is pre-publish scoring. A single pin that scores green across all 5 dimensions — good image, relevant board, keyword-rich title, optimized description, proper hashtags — outperforms 10 pins posted without any optimization.

Most creators spend hours on design but seconds on the title and description. That’s backwards. Pinterest’s search algorithm weights text just as heavily as the image.
