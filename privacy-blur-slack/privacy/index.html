Privacy Policy — Privacy Blur for Slack

Effective Date: 2026-07-19 · Last Updated: 2026-07-19

1. Who We Are

Privacy Blur for Slack is operated by Amrita Labs, based in Canada.
Contact: hello@amrita-labs.com

2. What This Extension Does

Privacy Blur for Slack blurs sensitive content (messages, sender names, channel lists, and media) on Slack Web during screen sharing, video calls, and presentations. It operates entirely client-side within your browser.

3. Data We Collect

We collect minimal data:

We do NOT collect:

  • Slack message content — we cannot read your messages
  • Sender names, channel names, or any text content
  • Browsing history or page URLs
  • Screenshots or screen recordings
  • Any personally identifiable information
  • Analytics or usage telemetry sent to external servers

4. What Leaves Your Browser

Almost nothing. This extension operates 100% client-side. The only external communication is:

  • Payment processing: When you choose to upgrade, ExtensionPay communicates with Stripe to process payment. Your email (if provided for trial) and payment details are handled by Stripe — we never see your card number.
  • chrome.storage.sync: Your preferences sync across your Chrome browsers via Google's built-in sync infrastructure. This is a Chrome platform feature, not our server.

We have zero backend servers. No Slack data, message content, or user behavior is ever transmitted anywhere.

7. Data Retention & Deletion

  • Preferences: stored in chrome.storage.sync — deleted when you uninstall the extension or clear extension data
  • Payment records: retained by Stripe per their retention policy
  • We retain no server-side data about you

To delete all data: uninstall the extension. All local data is immediately removed.

8. Children's Privacy

This extension is not intended for users under 13 (COPPA) or under 16 in the EU (GDPR). We do not knowingly collect data from children.

9. Security
